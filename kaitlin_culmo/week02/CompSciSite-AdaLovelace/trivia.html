ADA LOVELACE

TRIVIA

One of her tutors, the English mathematician Augustus de Morgan, warned her mother that Lovelace’s capacity for scientific thinking could effect her health: “The very great tension of mind which [mathematics] requires is beyond the strength of a woman’s physical power and application.”

She journaled after observing Babbage's machine, “The sciences of operations, as derived from mathematics mores especially, is a science of itself, and has its own abstract truth and value” - which is the first description of the science of computing.

Her "Notes" languished in obscurity before the book was re-published as part of English scientist B V Bowden’s 1953 book "Faster Than Thought: A Symposium on Digital Computing Machines". The onset of “electronic brains,” according to Bowden, had more in common with Lovelace’s genius foresight than with Babbage’s mechanical Engine.

The “vast and powerful language” she predicted is now the programming languate called ADA which powers underground trains, Boeing777s, satellites, and rockets.
